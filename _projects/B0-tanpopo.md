The cover photograph of the dandelion seed head was taken near the Kamo River (鴨川) in Kyoto in 2025. The pappus parachute shown above was photographed at Shinjuku Central Park (新宿中央公園) using an iPhone. As my daughter blew away more than a hundred of these delicate parachutes, I am lucky to preserve one. The background is removed for visual clarity.

A research team at the University of Edinburgh found that the dandelion pappus creates a separated vortex ring [(SVR)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-018-0604-2). The SVR forms above the pappus rather than attaching to it at terminal velocity, and it remains steady instead of shedding periodically. These two features have raised my interest in conducting a detailed computational study this year.
