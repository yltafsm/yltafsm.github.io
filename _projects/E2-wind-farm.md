Computational turbulence research at the wind-farm scale remains a significant challenge, with no universally established starting point and a wide range of approaches across the field. My research follows a direction initiated during my Ph.D. studies. I plan to continue along this path, systematically developing the necessary methods, until a comprehensive framework is established that enables reliable and efficient simulations at the wind-farm scale.

The Carrier-Domain Method (CDM) has been developed as an efficient computational framework for resolving flow evolution in the streamwise (flow propagation) direction. However, additional methods are required to provide flexibility in resolving the flow field in the crossflow direction.
